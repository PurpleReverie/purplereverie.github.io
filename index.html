Taura J Greig

I build software with a game developer's mindset and an engineer's discipline. Over the past eight years, I've worked across games, AR/VR, and full-stack platforms, taking ideas from rough prototypes to shipped, production-ready systems.

I'm drawn to problems that require both technical depth and creative thinking — designing gameplay systems, building tools that empower other developers and artists, and engineering backends that quietly do their job well. I enjoy owning projects end-to-end, making sense of messy codebases, and turning complex requirements into clear, maintainable solutions.

Lately, I've been leaning more into research-driven work, exploring AI and procedural systems while continuing to build practical software that's meant to be used, not just admired.

How I build

Ownership in ambiguous projects

I step into ambiguous projects to move progress forward and improve systems that deliver real value to stakeholders.

Systems-first thinking

I prefer building end-to-end systems, focusing on the problem space, business needs, and how a system will evolve over time before writing code.

Simple code until complexity earns its place

I default to clean, maintainable solutions and introduce abstraction only when necessary, guided by an understanding of scope, scale, and direction.

Enable others through tooling

I translate vague or non-technical requirements into clear, buildable systems that help designers, artists, and stakeholders do their best work.
